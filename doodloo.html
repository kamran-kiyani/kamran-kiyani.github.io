Project Working Title: Doodloo

Target Audience:

Primary Audience: Children between the ages of 3 and 8
Secondary Audience: Older children and possibly also adults

What does the audience value or appreciate?

Children like playful experiences. We are hoping to create enjoyable ‘play’ by allowing children to draw objects on paper and animate them on a screen.

How would you be able to tell whether your project was successful (for the audience)?

  1. If children between the ages of 3 and 8 spend a lot of time (undefined?) on the application and ask for repeat access, it will indicate that they enjoyed it.
  2. If we observe children using the application for behaviors beyond those demonstrated to them, it will indicate a willingness to explore its limits.
  3. If we observe children talking about their experience in real-life terms (e.g. motion in earth vs space), it will indicate a transfer of learning.

What would differentiate a high-quality from a low-quality (but "successful") version of your project?

  1. Completeness (how many milestones we are able to complete)
  2. Comprehensiveness (which features of the project we are able to implement successfully)
  3. Conjurorship (how much it surprises those playing it for the first time)

The region and setting

  • Saidpur, a quiet village by the Margalla hills
  • Along the caravan route from Peshawar to Delhi
  • Located in Potohar, a dusty backwater region north of the Punjab
  • Inhabited by tribes loyal to the Mughal emperor
  • Famous towns along this route: Rawalpindi, Lahore, Amritsar, Delhi.
